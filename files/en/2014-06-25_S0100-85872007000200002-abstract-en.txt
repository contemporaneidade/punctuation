The discussion presented in this paper turns around the several possible meanings of the qualifying adjective  popular  The charismatic figure of Father C cero Rom o Batista  Juazeiro s patriarch  has belonged  for many decades  to the pantheon of popular devotion of millions of pilgrims  but it is nevertheless still regarded with strong reserve inside the official Church  At the moment  it seems that this official position is being reviewed  An institutional rehabilitation of the  godfather  may well being under way  This being the case  the paper asks if  to what extent and under what conditions  can this transformation of Father C cero s image flow together with certain metamorphosis of what has been named  Popular Church  so as to endow  even though unexpectedly  the Brazilian Catholicism with a new icon
