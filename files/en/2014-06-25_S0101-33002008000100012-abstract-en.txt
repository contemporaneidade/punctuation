Contemporary architecture is caught in the risky fusion of marketing and the entertainment industry  The exploration of architectural form pushes the limits of materials to the point of inverting the principles of construction and production  in a play of volumes and effects apparently without rules and limitations  in a search for the highest possible rent  This article interprets this new condition of architecture   from the formation of fetish and fictitious capital to the building site   focusing on the production of Frank Gehry  The analysis of form  techniques  and materials allows us to investigate why cutting edge architecture is today one of the vanguards of the rentist economy
