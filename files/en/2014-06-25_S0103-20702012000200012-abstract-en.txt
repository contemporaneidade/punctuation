The article explores the relevance of the colour race variable in contexts of poverty  It sets out from an analysis of data taken from the research study  Associativism and social networks  conditions and determining factors in the access to social policies by lowincome populations  conducted by the Metropolitan Studies Centre of the Brazilian Analysis and Planning Centre  The study in question included two surveys  one in the district of Cidade Tiradentes  in S o Paulo Municipality  and the other in Bairro da Paz  in Salvador  both of which investigated aspects relating to socioeconomic composition  sociability  employment and access to public services abd benefits  Consequently it provides valuable input to the debate on the theme of relations between race and class in the understanding of inequalities
