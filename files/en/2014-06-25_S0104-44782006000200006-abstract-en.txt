The goal of this article is to demonstrate the relationship between the growth of global civil society and international public Law  on the one hand  and on the other  the importance of international regimes from the perspective of non State actors acute  participation in international relations  Debates in the literature on international regimes point to the need for the realization of more empirical research on the factors that condition the success of international regimes  although it is already possible to present evidence regarding one important role that has been occupied by non sovereign actors in regime formation  the role of specialists  This article is a reflection on the concept if  international regime  and its boundaries  It presents a preliminary note for research on the possibilities that civil society actors have for influencing the formation of an international regime for the environment  emphasizing Brazil acute s participation in the process of formulation of a regime of bio security
