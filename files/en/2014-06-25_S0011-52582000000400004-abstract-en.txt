This article analyzes the activity of the political parties in the Brazilian House of Representatives during the first Fernando Henrique Cardoso Administration  1995 98  The main source of data is the result of head count votes  Three aspects are emphasized  the degree of party discipline  the parties 146  rate of support for bills sponsored by the Administration  and the impact of maverick voting and absenteeism on the voting results  The link between the two dimensions treated separately by the literature  party discipline and party support for the Administration  shows that the strategy of evaluating Presidential power simply by counting the members of Representatives formally belonging to the Executive 146 s constituency should be done with caution in countries with undisciplined parties and high absenteeism during head count votes
