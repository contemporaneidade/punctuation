This essay explores the potential of the idea of fractality along one of its properties  scaled self similarity  The notion of fractal personhood is proposed as a way of thinking about the Amerindian person  When speaking of fractal personhood what is meant is the containment of whole persons in parts of persons and the replication of relations between selves and alters at different scales  intra personal  inter personal and inter group  Three Amerindian ethnographies  Arawet  Wari  and Tupinamba  are compared and analysed combining propositions from Melanesian exchange theory and a theory of Amerindian perspectivism in order to elicit the usefulness of  fractal personhood  as an analytical tool
