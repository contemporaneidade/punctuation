This article presents an analysis of the representations of the priestesses of the sun   the acclas   transmitted in the chronicles of three Spaniards of the sixteenth s century  the Jesuit Jos  de Acosta  the Crown s official Sarmiento of Gamboa and the mercedarian friar Mart n de Mur a  It is a study of statements that identify such women sometimes with the sacred  other times with the profane  and they disclose the Spanish conceptions of gender and religion that presided over the construction of the differences  inequalities  hierarchies and relationships among Incas and Spaniards  and among men and women  in the process of colonization of the Incas
