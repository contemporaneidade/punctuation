This paper presents a study on the urbanization process in S o Paulo on one of its more contradictory districts  Morumbi  Currently  the Morumbi region has the second largest slum in Sao Paulo  Parais polis  the fifth in Brazil and fourth in Latin America  Given the complexity of region neighborhood in socioeconomic  political  cultural and environmental terms  in this paper  data are analyzed according to two articulated movements  The first  more general  depicts the scene of its landscapes  the changes over the past 30 years  regarding both the verticalzation of housing for the upper and middle layers of the population  and the creation of points of luxury consumption and modern office towers  The second movement depicts the expansion and growth of its slums  analyzing existing sociability in residents  associations of Parais plolis and major social projects that are developed there
