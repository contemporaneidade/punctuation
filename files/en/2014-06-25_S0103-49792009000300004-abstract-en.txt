Since 1970  the Suy  an Indigenous people  inhabitants of the Xingu Indigenous Park  MT  has been experiencing a demographic recovery  as well as it has been happening with other Brazilian Indigenous peoples  Recent studies analyse the reasons that have led to a reversion of the long depopulation process undergone by Indigenous peoples in Brasil  The conjecture that the Suy s demographic recovery was due to social and cultural changes that reflected in their reproductive behavior was the reason for this study that describes their fertility levels and patterns between 1970 and 2007  Data is based on continuous vital statistics and field research  Results analysis is grounded on their sociocultural system and historical knowledge of the Suy s reproductive health
