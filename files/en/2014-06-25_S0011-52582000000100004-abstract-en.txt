In the 1980s  a conservative movement for public sector reforms attempted to adjust civil servants 146   alleged  egotistical  amoral behavior to the efficient achievement of collective goals  in accordance with the principles of Adam Smith 146 s invisible hand  Based on Karl Polanyi 146 s and Max Weber 146 s classic works on the establishment of the market and of modern bureaucracy  respectively  the article endeavors to show how the conservative approach errs by ignoring the specificities of bureaucratic organization as well as the socially constructed character of the market mentality  The conclusion is that public sector reforms based on the assumption of self interest end up breeding suspicion and fostering precisely the corrupt behavior that they are meant to forestall  thereby reinforcing the State 146 s incapacity to properly manage its actions in the social sphere
