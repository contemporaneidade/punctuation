The social sciences over the last few decades have rediscovered the relevance of space as an analytic category  The article discusses this  spatial turn  from the perspective of Brazilian social thought  framed here as a form of peripheral imagination  Analyzing the long history of reflection on space among Brazilian intellectuals and writers  I suggest that these classic writings on the land and Brazil s sert o region provide important material for thinking of spatiality in contemporary society in a non Eurocentric way
