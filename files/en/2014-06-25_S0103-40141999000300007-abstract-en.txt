THE MAIN goal of this article is to describe in a detailed way the connections between power  censorship and memory  during the final stage of Pombal s regime  Its mains source are the written records of the Real Mesa Cens ria  Royal Censorship Board  the institution of censorship that Pombal created to replace the tripartite censorship that lasted for more than two centuries  A voyage to some of its reports will allow us to understand their reports as an attempt to control what should be remembered and what should be forgotten  but also in what ways it should be remembered or forgotten  Finally  these reports will be understood as documents that record an intellectual task  objects for reading and interpretation  and part of a broader dialogue between power and culture
