This paper attempts to outline the multiple dimensions of the dialogue between Roger Bastide and the Brazilian modernists  especially M rio de Andrade  Following the concerns of the Brazilian  modernist  school  Bastide establishes his contact with the country   with the arts  the literature and the folklore   reconsiders M rio de Andrade s interpretations about Aleijadinho and the baroque  and especially  shares the formulations of this  modernist  leader about the  authenticity of the Brazilian culture  The author seeks to show that Bastide  through this interlocution  has earned his place as a foreign interpreter of Brazilian culture and society
