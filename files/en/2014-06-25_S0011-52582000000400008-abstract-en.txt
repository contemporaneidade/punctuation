Since the 1950s  agricultural structures in the State of Cear  have undergone profound changes  including both the growth of small farms on the one hand and the development of indirect or precarious occupation of land on the other  One consequence of this restructuring has been the impoverishment of small farmers  who have faced increasing difficulty in access to land  which in turn has aggravated the conditions for the social reproduction of this social sector  Based on data from the Brazilian Census Bureau  IBGE  the article describes these changes before attempting to identify two types of factors that have helped increase pressure on the land market  the mercantilization of farming land  causing small farmers to be forced off the land  and the first phase of a demographic transition involving decreased mortality
