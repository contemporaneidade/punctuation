In response to Fredric Jameson s argument  this conference argues that the peculiarity of the historical novel has been to elude any stable stratification of high and low  Its evolution exhibits rather an oscillating continuum of registers  and it s the extent of this continuum that arguably sets it apart from other narrative forms  The text also investigates the reasons why the historical novel  nowadays  is widespread among the superior levels of fiction  even more than during its classical period  the first decades of the XIXth century
