This article analyzes the C rio  candle lit procession  and the Feast of Our Lady of Nazar  as a great collective rite that combines formal and devotional aspects with the profane informality of a Brazilian festival  As a grass roots manifestation of Catholicism and a typical celebration of the state of Par  the C rio of Nazar  and attending festivities embrace native elements to express a regional identity  a particular temporality  and a perception of order and differing ways to contrast it
