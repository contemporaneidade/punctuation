Along this article  we sought to apprehend the logic underlying the triangular relation between international economic powers  structural reforms of the State  and democracy in Latin America  The study is based on the findings expressed in the Report on  A Democracia na Am rica Latina  Rumo a uma Democracia de Cidad s e Cidad os   Democracy in Latin America  towards a Democracy of Female and Male Citizens   PNUD  2004  The data subject to scrutiny refer to three States   Uruguay  Brazil and Honduras  Two hypotheses have been tested   1  structural reforms of the State in Latin America have been conditioned by the dynamics of the international economy  and  2  the democracy that is possible in Latin America is a formalist democracy  and it does not inhibit the rational choice of market oriented economic agents  The conclusions of this work are discussed in the light of political theories and international relations
