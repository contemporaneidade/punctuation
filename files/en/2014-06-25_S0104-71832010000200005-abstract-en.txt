This article analyses the relations among children  the dead and death  and the eventuality of a child s own death  to the Mebengokre Xikrin  an indigenous population living in North of Brazil and speaker of a G  language  These themes are developed on analyzing the fabrication of the body  the formation of the self and the person  and the relations with the dead  giving special attention to children  Thus  the formation of the self and children s relations to the cosmos and the dead   their eventuality of being captured by spirits of the dead  their being adopted in the after life  their mourning  their bodily adornment and painting  how they are cared in life preventing death  their bodies and social interactions   are analyzed in order to discuss in a innovative way mebengokr xikrin notions of childhood
