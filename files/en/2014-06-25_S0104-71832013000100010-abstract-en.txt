Each year men and women from Aracatu  a small city in northeast of Brazil  close their homes and move over thousand miles south to harvest coffee in Minas Gerais and S o Paulo States  As soon as coffee harvest finishes four months later they go back home and resume work in their own gardens  The money earned in the coffee harvest provides assurance consumption throughout the year  allowing devote to their activities on the family farm  This paper seeks to show how the wage labor done by those families reflects into their productive and reproductive dynamics  including acting in strengthening peasant character  in the sense of a peasant subjectivity  as was proposed by Klaas Woortmann
