This article brings some new elements to the discussion of the transformation of Brazilian evangelical context  For this  the text phocus on black music gospel    m sica negra  This kind of music is supported by contemporary musicalities as hip hop and reggae musical styles and is produced by independent groups  These groups are constituted by religious individuals  who are not connected to specialized musical enterprises  They present goods and services that are in a dialogical link with religious notions  particularly those related to afrodescendants religious adepts  Their practices and conceptions define a specific kind of participation and belonging  This perspective of study has as theoretical resources notions like  blackness   Black Atlantic  and  diaspora
