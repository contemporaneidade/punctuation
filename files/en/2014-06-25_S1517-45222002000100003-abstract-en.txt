The work analyzes the main transformations in the rural Social Welfare in Brazil  The outcome of these transformations has been the inclusion of rural hard working women in the welfare system as of the legislation approved by the National Congress in 1988  Rural work women in Brazil have become entitled to the benefits of paid maternity leave and retirement accordant to a legal age limit  Concurrently  the article examines the role played by the State and the civil society in the unfolding of the legislation related to rural Social Welfare  in an attempt of exposing its character of either a  donation  given by the State or the workers  own  conquest  Finally  the author queries the impact of rural Social Welfare implementation in the South of Brazil  emphasizing its achievements in the decrease of rural poverty and unequal income distribution  as well as its material and symbolic importance in the gender relationship shift in rural areas
