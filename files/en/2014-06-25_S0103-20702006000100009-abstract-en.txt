This article discusses the effects of extending the duration of school education among the working class  focusing on the specific case of two generations of steelworkers from the ABC region of S o Paulo  those who were students at the end of 1970s and those at school at the end of the 1990s  Field research was conducted in the Mercedes Benz factory  using direct observation  archival research and 52 biographical interviews  The prolongation of school education among working class youngsters is experienced ambivalently by parents and children alike  The hopes for career advancement nurtured by these youngsters have failed to materialize  despite the increase in schooling  Furthermore  the difference between the school experiences of parents and children seems to have led to a series of conflicts between the two generations
