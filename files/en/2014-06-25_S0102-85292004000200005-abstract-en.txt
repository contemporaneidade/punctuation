This paper uses descriptive method to analyse the origin and the seemingly intractable but avoidable ancillary factors that plunged C te d Ivoire  which was once the island of political stability  the showcase of economic prosperity and success in West Africa in the fangs of civil war  The paper surveys the policies of the colonial and postcolonial state till the death of President F lix Houphou t Boigny  the post Houphou t era and its lot of political and socio economic upheavals and the mismanagement of the legacies of Boigny that led to the internal conflict  The consequences of the civil strife are also surveyed at the national and sub regional levels as well as the resolutions of the conflict through the botched Accra peace initiatives that inexorably led to the Lina Marcoussis peace talks  The aftermath of the negotiations and the involvement of extra African powers and the future of democracy in C te d Ivoire are also looked into
