The objectivity and clarity of Marcel Gautherot s photographs of Bras lia  combined to the remarkable presence of thick shadows and sharp perspectives  bring them close  from a formal point of view  to the early works of the Italian painter Giorgio de Chirico  The article measures up Gautherot s artistic perspective against the intentions of architects Lucio Costa and Oscar Niemeyer and the literary and critical interpretations that describe the city as a  methaphysical  and  surrealist  landscape
