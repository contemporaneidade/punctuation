The aim of this paper is to present the results of an electoral research conducted in the University of S o Paulo by the Center of Studies in International Negotiation  CAENI  and the Junior Institute of Social Research  IJPS  during the 2010 Brazilian presidential elections runoff  In this work we discuss the sampling process and its implications on a universe of higher scholarization  We show that the prospective behavior is easier to isolate in situations like these  in comparison with classical surveys over the total Brazilian population
