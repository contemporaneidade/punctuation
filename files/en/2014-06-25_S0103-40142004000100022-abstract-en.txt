THE AUTHOR analyzes the political movement through which poor  black men manifested their discontent with the Portuguese monarchy and the proslavery society of Bahia in 1798  The regionalism of Bahia contributed to the exalted status of this movement  known then as Inconfid ncia Baiana  Insurrection of Bahia  which was more radical in advocating the independence of Brazil and more republican than the Inconfid ncia Mineira  Insurrection of Minas  because it embodied the yearnings of colonial Brazil s subordinate classes
