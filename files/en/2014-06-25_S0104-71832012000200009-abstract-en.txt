This paper examines the craft of baianas de acaraj  and tacacazeiras in the context of Iphan s heritage and safeguarding process  Focusing on the production and consumption of acaraj  and tacac  and using the concept of  culinary systems  the authors show that different conceptions of  heritage  circulate among these professionals  their clients and state agents  which has often lead to conflicts about the uses of urban space by these professionals  The ethnographic descriptions presented reveal how these specifics forms of foods occupy a central role in the formation of social and cosmological ties as well as of individual and collective subjectivities  The aim of the authors is to focus on the multiple representations and appropriations that are made of acaraj  and tacac  when these foods may appear as  typical   street food  or  holy food  in the processes of differentiation and formation of social belonging
