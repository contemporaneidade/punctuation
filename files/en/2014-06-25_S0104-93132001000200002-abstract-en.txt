This article discusses the differences and hierarchies found in the funeral rites of a peasant community in the southwestern region of the Colombian Andes  In this community   affected by different expressions of violence   funerals emerge as selective forms for the manifestation of social solidarity  In the population considered  marked qualitative and quantitative differences in the organization and evolution of these funeral rites were  in fact  observed  The differences are related to the sex and social position of the deceased  as well as to the way in which he or she died  At one extreme of the social practices and representations associated with death are the burials of young men killed tragically  These are the object of a particular cult  and are glorified by the community  At the other extreme  the community expresses its rejection of women who have committed suicide  an attitude which is particularly evident during the course of the funeral
