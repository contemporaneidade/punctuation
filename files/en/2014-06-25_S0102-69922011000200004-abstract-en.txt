Several themes that animate contemporary human sciences in their painstaking efforts to come to terms with society have been  in different ways  at the very center of the agenda of Brazilian social scientists  The following are some of such issues  emancipation  the right to difference  the limits to freedom  the definition of dignity as a social project  recognition  exclusion  among others  have all been subjected to the scrutiny of sociologists concerned with the issue of Brazilian national formation  This article intends to indicate some of the reflections that colored the 1950s and 1960s scholarly scene in both Brazil and Latin America  which anticipated most of such themes  despite their unsystematic theoretical treatments
