Amid rapid social changes in Brazil in the 1950s  wide reaching collective research projects in the social sciences were conducted in partnership between Brazilian and international agencies working in regional development  Cooperation between social scientists and government staff was based on the expectation that social and anthropological studies would orient the changes then under way  By approaching the research program in the S o Francisco River Valley  the article examines a turning point in the career of sociologist Donald Pierson  combining analyses of context and content in his intellectual production  Pierson concentrated initially on the institutionalization of the social sciences in Brazil  promoting a standard of sociological work based on scientific research  In his research in the S o Francisco River Valley  Pierson adopted a new approach by combining the sociologist s role as scientist with that of agent of social change  thereby highlighting the importance of the application of sociological knowledge in practice
