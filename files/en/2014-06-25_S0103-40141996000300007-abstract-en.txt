This paper discusses the first two years of Brazil s current stabilization programme   known as the Real Plan   and in particular its effects on the country s external economic relations  in the ligth of similar stabilization programmes adopted in Mexico from 1988 to 1994 and in Argentina since 1991  Although each national experience has its peculiarities  it is shown that the Brazilian economy has been reliving the Mexican and Argentinian experience in some important respects  combining success in terms of inflation reduction  with strong and persistent exchange rate appreciation  high deficits in the balance of payments on current account and dependence on volatile international capital flows
