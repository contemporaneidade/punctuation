The article examines the issue of cultural heritage  focusing on the process of negotiating reality  It draws attention to aspects of divergence and conflict deriving from the different values and interests of the actors involved  A number of examples are cited   such as the registration of the candombl  terreiro  Casa Blanca  in Salvador  Bahia  and the paradigmatic case of Copacabana   in order to demonstrate that public heritage policies cannot be dissociated from the heterogeneity and complexity of social life
