This article attempts to discuss a series of matters referring to the urban context of the city of S o Paulo  Its aim is to determine  a  how the main private groups are organized in order to define the spatial location of the modern tertiary sector  b  how their organizations pressure public powers in order to obtain infra structural benefits on urban equipments  c  which are the concrete answers of public powers  and d  which are the main social groups  originating in the lower classes  more affected by this struggle of interests
