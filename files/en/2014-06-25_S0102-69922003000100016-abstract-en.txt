The present article analyzes the territorial results of the environment and development policies and the territorial organization of the Brazilian Amazon until 1970  The first vector of this process lies in the territorial configurations of the public policies  Quantitative and qualitative indicators emphasize the disparities between protected and productive Amaz nia  The relation between environmental programs  the building of infrastructures and productive systems is evaluated by taking into account the governmental and private investments  the growth of protected areas and the implantation of the new  ecological corridors
