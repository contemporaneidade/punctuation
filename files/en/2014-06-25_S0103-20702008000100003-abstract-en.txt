This research studied the social and professional experience of a group of 82 women born between 1940 and 1950  coming from high income groups from S o Paulo city  all ex students of three Catholic women s colleges  The main objective was to identify and compare the forms of socialization to which they were exposed in their socioprofessional training  Research methodology included fieldwork  questionnaires and biographical interviews with former lecturers  school directors and past students  The study s findings enabled a better understanding of the mechanisms through which socialization processes determine the different dispositions characterizing the former students of a particular teaching system  They also helped show how the outcomes of this  primary  socialization interact with later experiences  modifying  reconstituting or reproducing past social patterns and expectations
