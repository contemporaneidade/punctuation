This article analyses the ways in which labour and social movement activists accounts for the mobilisations experienced in the Volta Redonda city  during the decades 1980 and 1990  We verify the city s social representation presented in these accounts  Situated in the Southern region of the Rio de Janeiro State  this internationally known steel city has got a enduring history of labour organisation and collective action  Focusing in that case one can deal with the memory  space and social identities embeddedness  Guided by labour anthropology issues  the empirical data resulted from field observation and oral history methodology
