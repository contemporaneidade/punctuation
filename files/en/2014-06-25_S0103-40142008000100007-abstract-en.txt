Israeli society constitutes an interesting example of the religion of politics  that is  an example of how the political dimension of a society may acquire a religious aspect of its own and assume a sacred character  In the beginning of the Zionist experience  the religion of labor  the construction of a civic calendar and of a new national identity gained importance  From the mid 1970 s on  other factors have prevailed  They are  memories from the Ha Shoah  the construction of national martyrdom sites  and the myth of resistance until the last man  In this essay  the tracks taken by a civil society that feels the necessity of redefining the contours of its identity and remodeling its public memory  considering itself a national community  are emphasized
