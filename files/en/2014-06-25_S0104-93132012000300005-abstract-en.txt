The focus of the present article is ethnographic  aiming to describe the children of the catingueira  a semiarid region in Para ba state  highlighting their participation in social and family life  I reveal the contingencies these children face an daily life as well as adults  expectations regarding them  focusing on three ethnographic memories  an event in church involving a three year old child  the ritual presentation of new members to the community and a funeral procession for a  little angel   a dead child  Here I propose to think of small children as laughable  of older children apprentices and of childhood as a period of work and moral learning  The text reveals a universe in which children become agents who are essential for the proper functioning of the family unit and  more generally  of the whole community
