The article analyzes styles of Brazilian popular music that arose in the Sixties  reading them in terms of continuity and discontinuity vis vis bossa nova  The core argument is that  although the experiments in harmony of the bossa nova musicians influenced a number of songwriters of the following generation  the latter broke with the aesthetics of restraint pioneered by the former  which was in keeping with various other cultural forms of the period  such as concrete poetry and Oscar Niemeyer s architecture  Popular musicians of the later generation  from protest music to tropic lia  returned to the traditional Modernist view of Brazil as an inexhaustible trove of cultural information  archaic and contemporary  regional and universal  and to the Modernist aesthetics of excess
