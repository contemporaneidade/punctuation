Since the years 2000 the panorama of pilgrimage in Brazil has been increased with the addition of new routes of pilgrimage which are inspired in the Route of Santiago of Compostela  in Spain  Amongst these routes  five became quite known as Caminho da Luz  Light Path  Minas Gerais  Caminho do Sol  Sun Path  S o Paulo  Passos de Anchieta  Anchieta Path  Esp rito Santo  Caminho da F   Faith Path  Minas Gerais and S o Paulo  and Caminho das Miss es  Missions Path  Rio Grande do Sul  In this article we analyze these five routes  which have become part of touristic programs  from a comparative perspective while trying to understand the longstanding aspects and general tendencies of these events in present time as well as their peculiarities and modalities within each specific context
