This article surveys the implementation and results of a pilot project called C dula da Terra  1997 2002  and its continuity through the National Credit for Land  after 2003  in Brazil  These programs were inspired in a model called  market assisted land reform  financed by the World Bank and implemented in several countries  particularly in Latin America  starting in 1994 as an alternative to agrarian reform  which is basically active in Brazil today  Based on data of empirical researches it is argued that the C dula da Terra was not well succeeded in providing a model of access to land replicable on a larger scale  as the World Bank has been arguing  Furthermore  the article evaluates the developments of this model in the land policies of Luis In cio Lula da Silva s Government  2003 2010
