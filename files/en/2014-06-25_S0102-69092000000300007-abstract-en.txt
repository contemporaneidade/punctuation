The article shows that Jos  Bonif cio s concerns about the destructive use of natural resources  particularly forests  represent a basic and recurring issue in his political thought  His writings  produced between 1790 and 1823  reveal his permanent commitment to the 18th century European scientific school known as  Economy of Nature  This school is considered one of the major links that contributed to the later formation of  ecology  A deep reform regarding the use of nature in Brazil  overcoming the predatory exploitation inherited from colonialism  was one of the main aspects of Bonif cio s national project which contained precise indications of public policies necessary to reach this goal  His most important contribution was to show the existence of a causal relationship between slavery and environment destruction
