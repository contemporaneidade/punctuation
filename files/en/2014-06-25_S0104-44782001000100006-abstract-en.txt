A Unitarian thought has been prevailing in the choice and adoption of urban instruments and policies  It was induced by the option of inserting cities in the process of productive restructuring  in accordance to the requisites imposed by the globalization model  Given its naturalized action  this procedure mirrors  in fact  intentions that are strange to the place  and that are incorporated in the population s imaginary as a consensus  Which are the results provoked by these strategies in the production of urban space and in the exercise of citizenship  and how were they incorporated and vehiculated in the electoral discourse of the first round of municipal majority elections in Curitiba in the year 2000  This essay searches answers to this question
