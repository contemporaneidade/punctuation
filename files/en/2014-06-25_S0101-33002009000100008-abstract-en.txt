This article analyses how a particular question   the irruption of political power focuses among the ancient brazilian coastal Tupi   was treated both by Pierre Clastres and H l ne Clastres  Although her reflection continues the project for a political Anthropology  proposed by him  it is important to point out both the agreements and disagreements between the authors  in order to draw new directions to the debate they have initiated  In this sense  one intend to evaluate the most recent developments of the Tupi Guarani anthropology  considering peoples of the past  but also of the present time
