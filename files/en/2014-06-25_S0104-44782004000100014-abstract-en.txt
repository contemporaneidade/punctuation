This article provides a narrative of the 1964 military coup  as viewed from the barracks  Since the coup was a national movement  it sometimes becomes difficult to look at its regional political and military aspects  Here  however  we seek a local focus on the political process  taking the state of Paran  as case in point  This paper presents a summary of the military structure and the position of officials who served in the state s military organizations  The dynamics of the coup are described from the perspective of what went on in the barracks and presents  through interviews  the point of view of three generals who had active participation in the movement
