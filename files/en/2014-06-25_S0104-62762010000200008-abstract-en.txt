The aim of this present article is to make a characterization of the political culture in the metropolitan region of S o Paulo  by examining attitudes and perceptions about the political system  forms of citizenship and availability for the associations and political action  It discusses two competing hypothesis  but not excluded  1  The classic question of a sociological nature of the relationship between socioeconomic context and urban culture and politics  and 2  the consideration of this process as influenced by local and national history and its connections  It emphasizes the political experience of citizens as independent variable in relation to political culture and that presents a causation between the cross institutional framework and the assumption by which agents formulate their preferences
