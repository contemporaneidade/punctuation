IF  ON one hand  the continuous increase in the number of women in the labor market consolidates their active economic presence in society  on the other  gender based inequalities are exacerbated  as reflected particularly in their historically higher unemployment rates  This essay attempts to characterize unemployment among women  who currently account for more than half of the unemployed  and to introduce some issues pertaining to the fuzzy line that separates them from inactivity  comparing their situation vis vis men s in the labor market of the S o Paulo Metropolitan Region
