This article analyzes the building of professionalism among members of the S o Paulo State Supreme Court  focusing on their social profile and career patterns as related to the ideas expressed by associate justices in the journals Revista dos Tribunais from 1912 to 1978 and Revista de Jurisprud ncia do Tribunal de Justi a do Estado de S o Paulo from 1967 to 1997  The objective is to assess identity trends in S o Paulo judges over the course of the 20th century  using discourse analysis  The study follows the social morphology of judges beginning with the creation of the S o Paulo Court of Appeals in 1873  analyzing whether there is a causal nexus between the group s profile and predominant ideology
