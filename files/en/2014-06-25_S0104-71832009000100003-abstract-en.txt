This article analyses a peculiar and obscure episode in the colonial history of southern Angola  In the 1880s  the Portuguese government sponsored the settlement in the Hu la plateau of a group of impoverished migrants from the island of Madeira  This was part of a strategy of guaranteeing territorial control  frontier expansion and definition of borders in the context of the  European scramble for Africa  The study of primary sources reveals a great deal of improvisation  fragilities and ambiguities that the colonial history written in the 20th century rapidly erased and replaced for a narrative of pioneering heroism and white entitlement
