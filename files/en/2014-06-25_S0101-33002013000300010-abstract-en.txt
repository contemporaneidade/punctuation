Dramaturgy in S o Paulo in the 1960s was marked by a close articulation of culture and politics and by changes in the social composition of the stage  The article examines how playwrights Leilah Assump o and Consuelo de Castro addressed the bewildering experiences of their female protagonists and the obstacles they faced   material  professional  sexual  ethical   and  instead of isolating their characters in the narrow frame of the so called  women s condition  how they sought to stage  in plays such as Fala baixo sen o eu grito and   flor da pele  gender relations
