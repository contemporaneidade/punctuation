This article discusses Fredrik Barths approach to the problem of ethnicity based on a wide ranging analysis of his work  Analysis focuses on various key ideas recurrent in the authors programmatic and theoretical methodological writings  as well as his ethnographic studies  A critical evaluation is made of the explicatory power of ecological and demographic factors in his model  the importance of his thesis of relative identity  the genealogy of theoretical influences found in Barthian explanations and the problem of the rational actor as the binding element in his interactionist conception of social relations
