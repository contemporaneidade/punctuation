This article aims at analyzing the main forms of housing precariousness in S o Paulo  that means houses in irregular buildings lots  shanty towns  slums besides precarious verticalized houses located mainly in the central area of the metropolis  Taking as reference the year of publication of the work S o Paulo 1975  Growth and Poverty  the authors discuss the hypothesis that the recent growth of the City of S o Paulo and the transformation of the housing panorama is reflecting not only the perpetuation of the old social differences but also increasing inequalities and segregation
