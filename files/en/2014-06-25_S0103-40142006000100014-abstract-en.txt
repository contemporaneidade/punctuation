BASED ON the work of Jos  da Silva Lisboa  the Viscount of Cairu  this article discusses the imaginary place of the Other in civilization  The objective of the article is to inquire into and explore the distance that separates civilized people from barbarians  In the intermediate space between civilization and barbarity  social literature plots out the frontier where civilized discourse revives fantasies of regression and poetically evokes the horror of a return to the jungle  This is a long literary tradition which  in Brazil  has Euclides da Cunha as its master
