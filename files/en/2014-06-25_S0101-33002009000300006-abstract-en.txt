This article takes the political conflict over the overthrow of the Brazilian Empire as key to understand the intellectual production of the first decade of the Republic  I argue that the intellectual debate at the time was genuinely a political one  opposing two relational identities   monarchists de plume  i  e  ex aristocrats  converted into knights of the defeated regime  and Jacobins  members of ascending social groups  working out the destruction of the former status quo symbols and the legitimization of the new ones
