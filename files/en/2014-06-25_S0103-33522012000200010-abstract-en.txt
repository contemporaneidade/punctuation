This article establishes a connection between institutional changes at local levels of government and local autonomy under federated political dynamics  To this end  the article examines how local governments in Brazil and South Africa gained administrative and fiscal autonomy by creating new municipalities  Focusing on the institutional incentives for those changes  the article identifies and analyses why the increase in the number of municipalities in Brazil and its reduction in South African affected the power of their respective local governments  The findings suggest that by using different processes to reinvent new local governments and creating intergovernmental institutions for cooperation  local political actors in both countries increased their power vis vis other levels of government
