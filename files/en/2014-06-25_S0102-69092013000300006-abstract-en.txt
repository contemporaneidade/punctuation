The article explores the concepts of origin that unfold in oral and written reports of the history and foundation of two settlements  one in the mid north of Mato Grosso  the other in the interior of Pernambuco  sert o  It examines the different values and meanings such narratives give to the past and the present  and how spatial  regional origin  and temporal  ancestral origin  references operate in complementary fashion in the production of relational identities and oppositions in those social universes  In focusing on native elaborations of the past   91 I 93  the author shows how differences of emphasis distinguish critical aspects of sociality  and how criteria for inclusion and exclusion activate networks of belonging  perceptions concerning politics and the state  and the senses and implicit limits to the notions of family and society mobilized in both social contexts
