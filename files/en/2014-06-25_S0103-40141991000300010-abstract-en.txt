The article purports to examine the ideas which the main characters of Cana  by Gra a Aranha  express on the relations between race and culture  as well as on the racial melting process and the future whitening of the population of Brazil in a brotherly and solar Utopia  These ideas are correlated  on one side  to the concern of Silvio Romero  Euclides da Cunha and Araripe Jr  with the adequation between geography  race and culture  on the other side  to the concern of S  Paulo s Modernist movement to define a Brazilian national character  This concern can be seen  on the level of symbolical representation  in Macuna ma and  on the level of a more systematic theorizing  in Portrait of Brazil  which goes so far as to mention eugenics when discussing the future Brazilian ethnical type
